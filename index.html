What is Cloud Computing?

Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services—including servers, storage, databases, networking, software, and more—over the internet ("the cloud") to offer faster innovation, flexible resources, and economies of scale.

Key Benefits

Scalability

Easily scale up or down your IT resources based on demand, saving costs and improving performance.

Cost Efficiency

Pay-as-you-go pricing helps organizations avoid the capital expense of buying hardware and software.

Reliability

Cloud providers offer data backup, disaster recovery, and high availability architectures.

Accessibility

Access data and applications from anywhere in the world with an internet connection.

Types of Cloud Services

  • IaaS (Infrastructure as a Service): Virtual machines, networks, storage (e.g., AWS EC2)
  • PaaS (Platform as a Service): Runtime environments, DBs, app hosting (e.g., Google App Engine)
  • SaaS (Software as a Service): Software over the web (e.g., Gmail, Office 365)
